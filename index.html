    Research

    All projects conducted under Aerospace and Mechanical Engineering Department, University of Southern California

    Multi-parameter Nonlinear Elasticity Mapping of Breast Masses

    PI: Pror. Assad Oberai Spring 2019 – Spring 2020

    Elastography or elasticity imaging refers to various techniques that distinguish between normal and diseased tissues by visualizing biomechanical properties of the diseased tissue.Though scientific techniques involving elastography vary significantly in detail, most involve ruffling the chosen tissue with a quasi-static, harmonic or transient mechanical source, measuring the respective displacement, and deducing mechanical properties of the tissue based on the data acquired.

    In this project, we calculated heterogeneity of patients’ tumors using shear modulus mapping of the tissue generated by the quasi-static approach and analyzed ROIs (Region of Interest) and regularization parameters’ effects on final heterogeneity outputs.

    Ultrasonic Medical Instrument

    PI: Prof. Anita Penkova Spring 2020 – Summer 2020

    Ultrasonic streaming is associated with propagation of sound in a particular fluid medium resulting from time-averaged nonlinear dynamics. It has begun to enter the biological field through sonochemistry and sonophoresis, where the ultrasound was used to stimulate or increase the absorption of drugs.

    In this project, the group is looking at the fundamental concepts of fluid dynamics of the acoustic streaming phenomenon with the main aim to predict the frequency and power output that the transducer needs in order to generate acoustic streaming fields that are able to accomplish the desired effects of existing biological applications.
